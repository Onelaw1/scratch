1. The Productivity Paradox

The "Zero-Sum" Fallacy

Policymakers often assume a linear relationship:
AI Adoption = Headcount Reduction.

However, empirical evidence suggests a J-Curve effect. While AI reduces task duration for routine activities (Substitution), it simultaneously lowers the marginal cost of production, leading to increased service volume demand and the creation of entirely new task categories (Generation).

The 3-Lens Impact Framework

Lens 1
Substitution (대체)
Tasks fully automated. Human role shifts to 'Reviewer'.
Lens 2
Augmentation (보완)
Human + AI. Quality increases, Time decreases.
Lens 3
Generation (창출)
New tasks created (e.g., Prompt Engineering, AI Ethics).

2. AI-Adjusted Dynamic FTE Model

Moving beyond static 'Volume × Time' to dynamic 'Impact × Probability'.

ƒ(x)
$$ FTE_{target} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left( \frac{V_i \times ST_i \times (1 - \alpha \cdot \beta_{ai})}{H_{year}} \right) + FTE_{new} $$
α (Adoption Rate) Organizational readiness factor
β (Suitability Score) Task-level AI feasibility index
FTE(new) Workload from new AI governance tasks

Step 1: Decomposition

Breaking down 1,200+ job positions into Atomic Tasks. Unlike traditional consulting which stops at 'Job' level, we analyze at the 'Action Verb' level to determine AI feasibility.

Step 2: Calibration

Applying the O*NET-based AI Suitability Matrix, adjusted for Korean Public Sector constraints (Security, Regulations).

Step 3: Simulation

Running Monte Carlo simulations to predict workforce needs under conservative, moderate, and aggressive AI adoption scenarios.

3. Strategic Implications for Management

Defensible Logic for Government Evaluation

When the Ministry of Strategy and Finance audits your headcount, "We are busy" is not a defense. "Our AI-ADFM analysis shows a 12% efficiency gain, which has been reallocated to field safety inspections" is a defensible, data-driven strategy.

02

From "Headcount" to "Skill-Count"

The conversation shifts from "How many people do we need?" to "What skills do we need?". Our system identifies the Reskilling Gap required to achieve the projected efficiency gains.
